Are you using AI chatbots for your mental health support?

We are inviting you to participate in a research exploring how people use AI chatbots to support their mental health.

Sign up for the study

Information about the study

People often turn to chatbots to discuss stressful situations at work, relationship struggles, or to find coping strategies for emotional or mental health concerns. This study aims to understand users' experiences, motivations, and perceptions when using AI chatbots to address their emotional or social needs.

A wide variety of chatbots are used to support mental health, ranging from general-purpose ones like ChatGPT and Gemini to more specialized such as Pi.AI, Dot, Replika, or Wysa. We are interested in hearing about any experiences, whether you've used chatbots for casual conversations or more focused mental health support.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Be aged 18 or older.
  • Interact with an AI chatbot at least once every two weeks over the past three months.
  • Speak English or Czech.
  • Be willing to participate in a 20-60 minute interview via chat, video, or in person.
  • Must not have any psychiatric diagnosis that may interfere with the interview and pose risk to your well-being.

Study Details

This study involves a one-time interview where you will share your experiences with AI chatbots. All responses will be kept confidential, and pseudonyms will be used to protect your identity in reports or presentations. The interview will be audio-recorded and transcribed, with both securely stored for four years before permanent deletion. Detailed information is available in the Informed Consent section on the sign-up page.
